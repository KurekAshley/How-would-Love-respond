# Governance

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to establish the governance standards for the Kurek Canon.

Governance exists to preserve the integrity, consistency, accuracy, and long-term stewardship of the philosophies, frameworks, methodologies, distinctions, and teachings contained within the Canon.

The Canon is intended to serve as the authoritative source of truth for all current and future works derived from this body of knowledge.

---

# Guiding Principle

The purpose of governance is not control.

The purpose of governance is preservation.

As the Canon grows, evolves, and expands, governance ensures that the original intent, meaning, principles, and teachings remain accurate and accessible for future generations.

---

# Definition of Canonical

A document, framework, distinction, story, model, methodology, or teaching is considered **Canonical** when it has been formally approved as an official part of the Kurek Canon.

Canonical content represents the authoritative version of that material.

Once designated Canonical, it becomes the primary reference source for all future use.

---

# Change Management

The Canon is intended to evolve.

However, changes should improve clarity without altering foundational meaning.

Changes may include:

* Clarification
* Expansion
* Additional examples
* Improved structure
* Improved explanations

Changes should not alter established definitions without deliberate review.

---

# Version Control

Major changes should be documented within:

```text
CHANGELOG.md
```

Each entry should include:

* Date
* Version
* Summary of changes
* Reason for change

# Preservation Policy

The Canon is intended to be preserved indefinitely.

All governance decisions should prioritize:

1. Accuracy
2. Clarity
3. Consistency
4. Integrity
5. Longevity
6. Accessibility

Short-term convenience should never compromise long-term preservation.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Governance exists to protect wisdom.

Its purpose is not to restrict growth.

Its purpose is to ensure that growth occurs without losing the essence of the teachings.

The Canon should evolve while remaining true to its foundational principles.
